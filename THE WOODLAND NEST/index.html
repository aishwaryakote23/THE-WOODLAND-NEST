The term Adivasi in fact is a modern Sanskrit word specifically coined in the 1930s by political activists to give a differentiated indigenous identity to tribals by alleging that Indo European and Dravidian speaking are not indigenous, and recognised by Markandey Katju the judge of the Supreme Court of India in 2011.In Hindi and Bengali, Adivasi means "Original Inhabitants," from ādi 'beginning, origin'; and vāsin 'dweller' (itself from vas 'to dwell'), thus literally meaning 'beginning inhabitant'

Our organization serves day and night for the welfare of the tribal people. We cater them with food, education and shelter for a peaceful living.
